    Virginia Tech, Computational Bioinformatics & Bioimaging Laboratory

    Virginia Tech's Computational Biology and Bioinformatics Laboratory (or CBIL, for short) is a well-funded research center that uses unsupervised machine learning and other statistical techniques identify clusters of genes which together control complex biological functions.

    Technology

    As an intern at CBIL, I built a desktop visualization tool called ModVis that displayed the output of many different module discovery algorithms in a way that allowed medical doctors to see and explore commonalities. Technologies included J2SE, Swing, AWT, and data from AffyMetrix MicroArray chips.

    Leadership

    In November of 2006, I published and presented my work at the 19th International Conference on Computer Applications in Industry and Engineering in Las Vegas.

    Toward the end of my tenure, I helped on-board a new graduate student who continued to work on ModVis.

    Entrepreneurship

    Though a long-shot, our driving hope was to identify clusters of genes that cause disorders like Muscular Dystrophy or Cancer when under- or over-expressed. Medical doctors could use that information to create 'healthy' viruses that would infect the affected cells, reverse the genetic anomaly, and cure the affliction.
